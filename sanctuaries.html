Featured Sanctuaries

Ananda Animal Sanctuary

Based just outside Biggar in Scotland, this vegan animal sanctuary is home to over 80 animals including sheep, goats, turkeys, cows, ducks, geese, pigs, chickens, cats and a three-legged dog. They rescue, rehabilitate and provide forever homes for animals that have been abused, neglected, saved from slaughter, or unwanted.

80+ Animals Biggar, Scotland
Visit Website

Tribe Animal Sanctuary Scotland

A small sanctuary for animals rescued from abuse, neglect or slaughter, located in the Clyde Valley. Founded by John and Morag, TASS provides sanctuary to factory farming victims and lets them live out their natural lives with compassion and respect.

Multiple Animals Clyde Valley, Scotland
Visit Website

Willows Animal Sanctuary

Founded in 1998, Willows is the largest ethically managed equine, farm and domestic animal sanctuary in Scotland. They operate a strict no-kill policy and care for almost 100 horses, ponies and donkeys, approximately 70 cats and dogs, and over 100 farm animals and birds.

270+ Animals Scotland
Visit Website

Farm Animal Rescue Sanctuary (FARS)

Established by Carole Webb in 1988, FARS is based near Stratford-Upon-Avon in Wolverton, Warwickshire. Currently home to more than 400 farm animals, with a peak of over 800 animals cared for. They provide a safe haven where animals can live freely, healthily and happily.

400+ Animals Warwickshire, England
Visit Website

Goodheart Animal Sanctuaries

Located on a 92-acre site in the beautiful Worcestershire countryside, Goodheart Farm Animal Sanctuary provides a lifelong haven for over 300 rescued farm animals. They work towards a better future for animals through compassion, education and advocacy.

300+ Animals Worcestershire, England
Visit Website

Hopefield Animal Sanctuary

Located on 53 acres of Essex countryside, Hopefield has been saving animals together since 1983. Home to over 100 species of rescued animals including meerkats, skunks, foxes, parrots, birds of prey, and farm animals. They provide a safe and loving home for abused, abandoned and neglected animals.

600+ Animals Brentwood, Essex
Visit Website

Lotus Lamb & Animal Sanctuary

A vegan-run animal sanctuary based in West Sussex, dedicated to rescuing, rehabilitating, and providing life-long care to animals who have suffered from neglect and abuse. They strive to find suitable forever homes for those who are adoptable and rely on community support to care for their over 200 animals.

200+ Animals West Sussex, England
Visit Website

Pear Tree Farm Animal Sanctuary

Our Somerset-based animal sanctuary began just before Christmas 2018, when we welcomed 3 huge pigs into our lives with open arms! They were booked in for the slaughterhouse, and we could not possibly have refused them a home. Since then, we have become home to a selection of beautiful souls, all saved from a harsh reality and death. Today we are an ever-growing family of rescues, and we have helped over 7400 animals find their way to the safety of loving, forever homes.

7400+ Animals Helped Somerset, England
Visit Website

Beneath the Wood Sanctuary

A Viva! award-winning vegan sanctuary based in Wales that provides permanent homes for dogs with behaviour issues, pigs, sheep, cats and horses. Founded by Sasha Bennett in 2009 on a 15-acre site in Ceredigion, nestled in beautiful rolling hills between Lampeter and Tregaron. In 2019, they were involved in a high-profile case that rescued #53 pigs from truly horrifying conditions at an illegal slaughterhouse in Pembrokeshire.

135+ Animals Ceredigion, Wales
Visit Website

Dean Farm Trust

Located just outside Chepstow, Dean Farm Trust is an animal sanctuary with a mission to rescue, rehabilitate and give a home for life to abused, neglected and unwanted animals whilst promoting compassionate living. Voted the UK's "Favourite Animal Sanctuary" in the Vegfest UK Awards in both 2020 and 2021.

200+ Animals Chepstow, Monmouthshire
Visit Website

Welsh Wildlife Centre

Rescuing and rehabilitating native Welsh wildlife, with educational programs about local biodiversity.

90+ Animals Pembrokeshire, Wales

Eden Farmed Animal Sanctuary

Located in Kells, Co Meath, Ireland, Eden is a vegan home where farmed animals who have been rescued from the animal use industries are given sanctuary for life. At Eden they are regarded with the dignity and respect that is their right. The sanctuary promotes animal rights and veganism, believing that all sentient beings have equal fundamental rights not to be bred, owned, used, exploited, harmed or killed.

Multiple Animals Kells, Co Meath, Ireland
Visit Website

Back Into Daylight

A non-profit animal sanctuary in Eastern Ireland that creates a safe and loving forever home for farmed and domestic animals in need. They care for over 600 animals including goats, pigs, cats, ducks, geese, dogs, pigeons, hens, cockerels, and small birds. They promote a vegan philosophy and encourage people to speak out against the use and abuse of animals in the farming industry.

600+ Animals Navan, Ireland
Visit Website

Heartstone

A veganic (vegan organic) animal sanctuary charity in Ireland dedicated to giving rescued animals a safe haven and a second chance at life. Their mission is to provide a peaceful refuge where animals can live out their lives feeling loved and cherished, roam the fields, and feel the sun on their faces. Heartstone also supports rewilding and promotes sustainability and a holistic, nonviolent way of living that respects all life.

Multiple Animals Co Meath, Ireland

How You Can Help

Volunteer

Give your time to help care for animals and support sanctuary operations.

Donate

Financial contributions help provide food, medical care, and shelter for rescued animals.

Spread Awareness

Share sanctuary stories and help educate others about animal welfare.

Shop Responsibly

Choose cruelty-free products and support ethical brands that don't harm animals.

Plan Your Visit

Book in Advance

Most sanctuaries require advance booking for visits. Contact them at least 2-3 weeks ahead to secure your spot.

Visit Times

Sanctuaries typically offer tours between 10 AM - 4 PM. Check individual sanctuary websites for specific hours.

Group Tours

Perfect for families, schools, or groups. Many sanctuaries offer educational programs and guided experiences.

Volunteer Opportunities

Consider volunteering during your visit. Many sanctuaries welcome help with animal care and maintenance.
